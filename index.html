The trade

What's actually being sold

Each session, at noon, the strategy sells an iron condor on the S&P 500 index that expires the same afternoon (0DTE). It sells one out-of-the-money call spread above the market and one put spread below it, collecting two premiums for the risk of one. If the index simply stays in a wide range into the 4:00 PM settlement — which it usually does — both spreads expire worthless and the credit is kept.

The short strikes are chosen by delta: roughly the option's probability of finishing in-the-money. A 16Δ short strike wins about 84% of the time; the research's central question is when to step down to the safer 12Δ or 10Δ strikes to avoid the rare day that bites.

Iron-condor payoff at expiry: a flat band of profit between the short strikes, with losses capped by the protective wings.

Glossary

0DTE
Zero days to expiration. The option is opened and expires the same afternoon. Time-decay is brutal in the final hours, which is the seller's edge.
Credit spread
Sell one option and buy a further-out-of-the-money option as protection. You collect a credit up front and keep it if the market stays on the safe side of your short strike. Max loss = (width − credit) × $100.
Iron condor
A call spread and a put spread at the same time. You collect both credits, and only one side can lose. Margin is one side's width, not two — so it's capital-efficient.
Delta (10Δ / 12Δ / 16Δ)
The short option's delta ≈ its chance of finishing in-the-money. 16Δ ≈ 84% expected win rate; 10Δ is further out — safer, but a smaller credit. The strike is picked each day from live implied volatility, so it moves with the market.
Noon entry
The trade is put on at 12:00 ET rather than at the open. Waiting out the morning's noise turned out to roughly triple P&L versus a 10:00 entry.
Account ROI
Total P&L ÷ the assumed $20,000 starting cash. The headline return.
Risk ROI
Total P&L ÷ the cumulative max-loss capital actually put at risk. A fairer cross-strategy comparison when position sizes differ.
Tail loss
A single session losing more than $500. Rare, but it's what kills a premium-selling account — so the research treats zero tail losses as a hard gate.
Regime
A market backdrop the strategy is stress-tested against: a calm bull grind, a crash-and-recover V, and a grinding bear.
Held to expiry
The position is carried to the 4:00 PM cash settlement rather than managed intraday. On 0DTE, hourly stops mostly just get whipsawed.
The test

One strategy, three very different markets

The same rules are replayed over three deliberately distinct backdrops. A premium seller that only works in a calm bull market is worthless; the bar is surviving all three.

What the research found

Four results that shaped the strategy

Finding 1 · Timing

Noon beats the open

Entering at 12:00 ET — after the morning's gap and news noise has played out — roughly triples account ROI versus a 9:30–11:00 entry, with far fewer losing days. Same structure, just a calmer starting point.

Account ROI by entry time, full-size 16Δ condor. Noon (highlighted) dominates.
Finding 2 · Structure

The iron condor is the workhorse

Selling only calls wins in flat-to-down markets but bleeds in rallies; selling only puts does the reverse. The condor collects both premiums and stays positive in every regime — the only direction-neutral structure that does.

Total P&L by structure and regime. The condor (blue) is green everywhere.
Finding 3 · Risk

Losses cluster on a few recognizable days

An autopsy of every losing and near-losing session shows the damage concentrates on reversal / chop mornings, high-volatility stretches, and low-confidence days. That's exactly what the delta-switch policies are built to sidestep.

Finding 4 · Discipline

Avoiding the tail beats chasing the return

The naive full-size 16Δ book actually books more raw P&L — but it does so by accepting a multi-hundred-dollar tail-loss day. The promoted champion gives up that sliver of return to push tail-loss days to zero. In premium selling, the tail is the whole game.

Method

How a candidate becomes the champion

Ideas don't get promoted because they scored highest once. Every theory runs through the same loop, and a candidate that breaks any acceptance gate is rejected — even if it makes more money.

What we learned

  • Volatility shock-decay buckets were built from local pre-entry rolling-vol history only: 17 high-vol sessions, 7 decaying high-vol sessions, 9 rising high-vol sessions, and 2 decaying/compressing high-vol sessions. Default16 decaying/compressing-bucket P&L was $213 with 0 tail losses and worst $105.
  • Best volatility shock-decay variant was vol_decay_restore16: $11,549, account ROI 57.7%, risk ROI 17.0%, tail losses 0, worst -$227.
  • Simple decaying-vol restore compared against fixed highvol12 at $11,549 versus $10,798; worst days were -$227 versus -$227.
  • Decay plus compression restore compared against fixed highvol12 at $10,868 versus $10,798; tail losses were 0 versus 0.
  • Adding a 10d veto for rising/expanding high-vol days changed P&L from $10,798 to $10,970; loss days were 1 versus 1.
  • Balanced volatility shock-decay gate passed for: vol_decay_highvol12_control, vol_decay_restore16, vol_decay_restore_compress16, vol_decay_restore_not_expanding16, vol_decay_rising10_restore16, vol_decay_compress16_rising10, vol_decay_expanding10_highvol12.
  • Leave-one-regime-out selected vol_decay_restore16, vol_decay_rising10_restore16 with 0 held-out tail losses and worst held-out day -$227.
  • Loop improvement: volatility records now persist prior-session rolling-vol slope and bucket P&L, so future vol theories can be tested without leaking same-day closes.
Honesty

What's been rejected, and what's next

Tested and set aside

  • path smoothness delta switch (path_shape) — Use 12d or 10d instead of 16d on smooth-trend mornings; keep 16d on chop or reversal.
  • credit per risk regime switch (option_surface) — Choose the farthest delta whose credit/risk ratio remains above a rolling or regime-neutral floor.
  • llm loss autopsy taxonomy (llm_native_proxy) — Generate autopsies, cluster failure modes, then only test rules that map to repeated clusters.
  • bad day cluster abstain (loss_autopsy) — Switch from return-seeking default16 to highvol12/highvol10 after drawdown until equity recovers.
  • flow oi disagreement filter (option_surface) — De-risk only when volume and OI agree directionally; fade or ignore one-sided stale OI.
  • nearest loss neighbor gate (loss_autopsy) — Use highvol12 or highvol10 when nearest-loser distance is below a fixed percentile.
  • range expansion then compression (path_shape) — Use 16d when range compresses into noon; use 12d/10d when range expands into noon.
  • pre trade memo risk rubric (llm_native_proxy) — Generate a structured memo per day and test policies driven by reason-code combinations, not raw thresholds.
  • delta credit curve kink (option_surface) — Select 12d when it captures most 16d credit with materially lower tail exposure; select 10d when the curve is flat.
  • volatility shock decay restore (volatility_regime) — Restore 16d on high-vol days when volatility is decaying and the morning range compresses; keep 12d/10d when volatility is still rising.
  • opening gap absorption switch (overnight_gap) — Use 12d on gap-continuation days; allow 16d when the gap is absorbed or compressing by noon.
  • morning reversal restore default (path_shape) — Permit normal 16d size on reversal days that the broad volatility overlay would otherwise de-risk.
  • regime diversified policy vote (meta_strategy) — Use a simple vote: select strategies that rank top two in at least two regimes and never breach worst-day gates.
  • synthetic scenario critic (llm_native_proxy) — Downshift delta when the plausible adverse scenario would breach 16d even if historical sample did not.
  • winner giveback restore (meta_strategy) — Temporarily restore default16 when highvol12 has underperformed default16 for several active days without losses.
  • am event digest classifier (event_digestion) — Trade AM events at normal size only when the post-event path compresses; otherwise shift to 12d or skip.
  • pm event premium exception (event_digestion) — Instead of hard skip, allow 10d quarter-size only when credit/risk clears a conservative floor.
